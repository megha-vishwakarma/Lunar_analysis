Why the Moon Matters

Ocean Tides

The Moon's gravitational pull creates Earth's ocean tides, which are essential for marine ecosystems and have influenced human navigation for millennia.

Stabilizes Earth

The Moon stabilizes Earth's axial tilt, providing a relatively stable climate which has been crucial for the development of life on our planet.

Space Exploration

As the closest celestial body to Earth, the Moon serves as a stepping stone for deeper space exploration and potential future colonization.

Lunar Fun Facts

  • The Moon is Earth's only permanent natural satellite, orbiting at an average distance of 238,855 miles.
  • It is the fifth largest moon in the solar system, with a diameter of 2,159 miles.
  • The Moon is slowly moving away from Earth at a rate of about 1.5 inches per year.
  • Only 12 people have ever walked on the Moon, all during the Apollo missions between 1969 and 1972.
  • The dark side of the Moon isn't actually dark; it receives just as much sunlight as the near side.
  • The Moon has quakes called moonquakes that are much weaker than earthquakes.
